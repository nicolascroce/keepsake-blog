Every time you use a tool, there's friction. Tiny unnecessary gestures. One extra click, a piece of info to re-enter, a tag forgotten.

Individually, it's nothing. But multiply that by 20, 50, 100 times a week, and it adds up. Worse: it slows you down exactly when you have an idea to capture, a task to log, a piece of information to file.

Keepsake 0.12 tackles those frictions. No visual revolution — but improvements that change how you interact with the app every day.

## Tags in one click, everywhere

Before, to add a tag to a task or an entry, you had to use the `#tag#` syntax in the text. It worked, but it wasn't very intuitive. (If you're new to tags and pages in Keepsake, [this page explains the concept](https://keepsake.place/en/quicknotes?ref=blog).)

Now, a **tag selector** is available in every creation and editing form — tasks, entries, notes. Click, pick, done.

And when you edit a task or entry directly in the list (quick edit mode), the tag selector is right there too. No need to open a separate form.

The contact and tag badges under your items update instantly. You see the result of your changes in real time.

## Batch select in the Inbox

If you have 15 notes to process, doing them one by one is tedious.

You can now **select multiple notes at once** in your Inbox. Checkboxes appear, and a floating action bar shows up at the bottom of the screen.

What you can do in a single action across the entire selection:

- **Copy** the content
- **Merge** several notes into one
- **Pin** or unpin
- **Add a tag or contact** to all selected notes
- **Convert to tasks**
- **Delete**

A "Select all" button lets you process your entire Inbox in one click. Perfect for your Sunday evening or Friday morning triage sessions.

## Your notes never lose anything

Three improvements that change how comfortable writing feels:

**Auto-save.** As soon as you start typing a new note, it's saved. If your browser crashes, if you accidentally close the tab, if your connection drops — your text is there.

**Contacts and tags from the start.** Before you even write a word, you can link a contact or a tag to your note from the bar under the input field. By the time you start writing, everything is already organized.

**Quick capture bar in focus mode.** You're deep into a long note in full-screen mode and an unrelated thought pops up? The quick note bar is always accessible at the top. Capture the idea on the fly, then get back to your writing.

## Convert notes without losing context

You took a quick note with a contact mentioned and a tag attached. Later, you decide to turn it into a task.

Before, contacts and tags were lost in the conversion. You had to add them back manually.

Not anymore. When you convert a note into a task or entry, **the mentioned contacts and associated tags are preserved**. Everything follows.

## T+ASAP and T+? for your tasks

You may know [the `T+3` syntax](https://keepsake.place/en/quicknotes?ref=blog) to create a task in 3 days directly from the quick note bar. Two new shortcuts join the family:

- **T+ASAP**: creates a priority task with no fixed date — for things to handle as soon as possible
- **T+?**: creates a "someday maybe" task — for ideas to keep without pressure

Concrete examples:
- `Reply to Marie's proposal T+ASAP` → priority task, no fixed date
- `Read the book Paul recommended T+?` → in the "someday" list, no pressure

## Clickable @mentions and companies

Contact @mentions are now **clickable everywhere in the app**. In a note, a task, an entry — click a name and you go straight to their profile.

And when you type `@`, your **[companies](https://keepsake.place/en/companies?ref=blog)** now appear in the suggestions too, alongside your contacts. An `@Synapse Technologies` takes you to the company profile.

## Fixes

- Text formatting shortcuts (strikethrough, highlight) now work on **Safari**
- Entries added on the same day are displayed in the correct **chronological order**
- Using the quick note bar while writing an entry no longer causes your text to disappear
- Unchecking a task during the 5-second countdown now works correctly
